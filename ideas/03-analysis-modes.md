# Analysis Modes

## Concept

Let users choose what kind of analysis they want after selecting a race/session context.

This avoids limiting the app to head-to-head comparisons and makes it useful for broader race understanding.

## Suggested Modes

### Race Overview

Explains the race as a whole.

Cards may include:

- Winner and podium
- Pole sitter
- Fastest lap
- Race result table
- Major race events
- Safety car, VSC, and red flag timeline
- Biggest movers and losers
- Main reason the race was won

### Head To Head

Compares two drivers.

Cards may include:

- Qualifying position comparison
- Race result comparison
- Sector comparison
- Speed trap comparison
- Telemetry overlay
- Track map
- Tyre strategy comparison

### Driver Deep Dive

Analyzes one driver.

Cards may include:

- Qualifying performance
- Race pace
- Stint performance
- Tyre degradation indicators
- Teammate context
- Result relative to starting position

### Strategy Analysis

Explains race strategy.

Cards may include:

- Stint lengths
- Tyre compounds
- Pit timing
- Winner's strategy baseline
- Alternative strategy possibilities
- Safety car strategy impact

### Telemetry Analysis

Focuses on speed, throttle, braking, and distance traces.

Cards may include:

- Speed profile
- Speed delta
- Throttle trace
- Brake trace
- Corner or zone hotspots
- Track map overlay

### Team Analysis

Compares teammates or team-level race execution.

Cards may include:

- Teammate finishing order
- Points scored
- Strategy split
- Qualifying gap
- Race pace comparison

### Beginner Explanation

Explains the race in plain language.

Cards may include:

- What happened?
- Why did the winner win?
- What does the tyre strategy mean?
- What is a safety car and why did it matter?
- Key F1 terms from this race

## UI Direction

After selecting year/race/session, show analysis mode as a segmented control or tab group.

Each mode should decide which cards are relevant. Avoid showing every possible card at once.
