# Race Intelligence Platform

## Concept

Evolve the app from a narrow driver-vs-driver comparison tool into an F1 race intelligence platform.

The app should help two audiences:

- New fans who want to understand what happened in a race without needing deep F1 knowledge.
- Existing fans who want fast access to telemetry, strategy, driver, team, and race-context insights.

## Product Positioning

The product should feel like:

> The place fans go after a race to understand what actually happened.

Head-to-head comparison remains useful, but it becomes one module inside a broader analysis product.

## Core User Flow

1. User selects a race context:
   - Year
   - Grand Prix
   - Session
2. User chooses what kind of analysis they want.
3. App loads structured race data and dashboard cards.
4. User can ask AI to explain the full race or a specific card.
5. User can vote, share, or save insights.

## Product Pillars

- Race overview
- Driver analysis
- Team analysis
- Strategy analysis
- Telemetry analysis
- Timeline and incident analysis
- Beginner-friendly explanations
- Social engagement through voting and shareable outputs

## Why This Matters

A pure chatbot interface can feel impressive but unreliable. A pure telemetry dashboard can feel too technical. The best version combines structured data, interactive dashboards, and optional AI explanation.

The app should not only answer questions. It should teach users how to read a race.
